# Week 13 Blog: Other "Open" Projects

This past week started off with an interesting exercise in researching other open source-minded projects that are not specifically in the tech space -- an area and sector that I had not previously considered in the context of this class. I had, however, interacted deeply with a few of these resources in the past, namely the "open" resource I picked: Genbank. A quick description, Genbank is a publicly accessible database of DNA and genetic sequence information that allows researchers, educators, and the public to freely access, submit, and analyze genetic sequence data. It is an invaluable tool to the fields of modern biology, medicine, and biotechnology, and it also shows how important it is to have open source ideas and projects in all areas of our society, highlighting the necessity of truly free access to information for everyone. 

I had previously interacted with Genbank to start development on a formatting converter between different structures of documents that can be submitted to and processed by Genbank and the NCBI, since certain entities and sites can only take certain file formats. I am hoping to complete and publish this converter in the next week! As for group contributions, they are running really quite smoothly, as my group already has a slew of valuable and significant contributions to Keycloak that we can be proud to present, and we are starting to work on the outline of our presentation.
