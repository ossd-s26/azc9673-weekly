# Week 11 Blog: The Cathedral and the Bazaar + Group Stand Ups and Continued Work

Our in-class discussions this week about "The Cathedral and the Bazaar" definitely opened my eyes to new perspectives on its core concepts of monolithic vs. decentralized software development and all of the key considerations that come with these ideals. A personally significant learning moment for me was when I suggested during class discussion that so long as many eyes are viewing a project and finding bugs or issues, AND many mouths are communicating these issues with those who may be able to easily fix them, there shouldn't be a need to consider potential issues that aren't found. Some examples of security risks and unnoticed gaps in wide developments that led to catastrophic failures that we later discussed strongly proved me wrong, and have surely helped me understand and continually remember the importance of routine and intricately detailed code checks and maintenence to avoid such disasters. 

During the second of the week's classes, every group had the opportunity to present their progress, potential issues, and any key points of interest to the rest of their peers. It was, as usual, hugely interesting and helpful for my work in my own group to hear all these updates, both to gain new perspective on how others are tackling their projects and making contributions, and to compare progress with our own group to ensure that we're on the right track with the right pace (which I believe we are). 

As for my own work for the project, I was able to make and test changes to fix the [Maven whitespace issue](https://github.com/keycloak/keycloak/issues/47499). While testing I also found a similar issue with apostrophes in the path/user, which I also fixed and added to the [pr](https://github.com/keycloak/keycloak/pull/47786).
