# Week 14 Blog: Closing out the Project and Personal Contributions

As we wrapped up our current contribution tasks for Keycloak and began preparations for the presentation this week, I can confidently say that I am proud of the work our group has accomplished in this short time. Not only have our contributions been meaningful, useful, and successful, we as a group have been able to work together incredibly smoothly, which I can only attribute to the considerations towards group work and collaboration that this class had us ponder before beginning this project to prepare us for this. The PR that I've been working on is good to go for now and just awaiting my partner Ben's PR to be reviewed and merged before drafting. Working on the presentation has itself been a nice exercising in summating our group work and highlighting each of our efforts towards improving Keycloak.

On the personal contributions side, I am also proud to publish a helpful tool for genetic and biological scientists, like my mom, working with Genbank and NCBI style files by allowing them to easily convert between the two. I've been working on the functionality on and off for some time at the request of my mom, and I am finally confident in its functionality and consistency. It even has a few users already! (my mom and her colleagues)
